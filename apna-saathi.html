01 — Problem

Older adults are isolated. Existing platforms don't serve them.

India has over 225 million people aged 55 and above. As children move to different cities and routines change post-retirement, a large segment faces social isolation. Platforms like Facebook and WhatsApp groups are not designed for their unique needs: accessibility, proximity, curated interests, and a sense of belonging.

Problem statement
"Older adults often face isolation and limited opportunities for meaningful community engagement. They seek platforms that offer tailored, accessible connections to stay active, pursue hobbies, and build lasting relationships. Existing platforms fail to address these unique needs."

04 — User Research

6 interviews + 26 survey responses shaped the product

Event type preferences
Cultural performances, health & wellness workshops, and spiritual gatherings were top preferences. 55–64 year olds preferred outdoor activities; 65+ preferred spiritual events.
Barriers to participation
Transportation and lack of event awareness were the top barriers. Those with health constraints preferred virtual events but still missed the personal touch.
Group size preference
Most preferred intimate groups (<20 people) for deeper engagement. This directly drove the event discovery and filtering design.
Tech comfort
Most users were active on WhatsApp and Facebook. Some needed family support for new apps — leading to a simplified OTP-based onboarding and family-assisted setup option.

07 — Product Scope

What's in and what's deliberately out

In scope (MVP)
Event discovery with personalised recommendations
Filter by category, distance, price, online/offline
OTP-based onboarding with hobbies setup
User profiles with photo, interests, about me
Ticketing, payment (UPI, GPay, Cards), refund
Reward points & discount code redemption
Social posts, likes, comments, sharing
Push notifications & WhatsApp/email reminders
Out of scope
Proprietary video/hardware infrastructure
Event logistics management for organisers
Tier 2/3 city launch at MVP stage
Health tracking or wearable integrations
In-app video calling (uses Zoom/Google Meet)

10 — Reflection

What building this taught me

🔍
Research shapes everything
The insight that 65+ users prefer spiritual events while 55–64 prefer outdoor activities directly changed the category taxonomy and home screen design.
⚖️
Scope discipline is PM work
Deciding what's explicitly out — proprietary video, hardware, Tier 2 expansion — was as important as defining what's in. It kept the MVP focused.
👤
Personas prevent assumption traps
KS Murthy and Maya had fundamentally different needs. Without them, we'd have built one app for two very different users — and served neither well.
